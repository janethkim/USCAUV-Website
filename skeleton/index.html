Underwater Robotics


The best thing since sliced breadboards.

Who are we?

We might be the most interesting and diverse group of individuals you'll ever meet in your whole life. Plus we get along in a way that's almost uncanny. Building robots is basically our whole life. If we're not in lab, we're thinking about being in lab. If we are in lab, we're kind of upset about it honestly. But we persevere. We solve bugs. We order new parts. We get things done. Once we hung out outside of lab. How are we so great? Some say it's luck. Others say it's just the magic of iLab.

Every year we compete in a competition put on by perhaps the most incredible orgnization known to mankind. In order to win, we have to get our robot to swim around, touch buoys, follow underwater paths, avoid sharks, rescue some treasured friends tied up at the bottom of the lake, all while avoiding the Giant Squid. Sounds impossible doesn't it? We thought so too. Then we tried to do it, and realized it really is pretty hard. But every day we're making great progress, and soon we'll be holding that Robotics Cup high to the cheers of friends and family, and that'll show 'em!

Sometimes life gets you down. The alarm steals you from the warmth of sleep and, blearily, you watch your reflection in the mirror. You stare at her, standing there, poised to brush her teeth. Who is that person? Why is she here? Was this how it was supposed to go? You started your life carefree and empowered. But time steals those moments away until before you know it, you're sitting in a cube under a 60hz pasty white glow and wondering where you went wrong. How can life be like this? Does everyone feel this way? Coffee for the road, coffee for the cube, a light lunch so you're not sleepy in the afternoon. Grim. But then comes that moment. Someone gives you a problem and says "Solve this." And you print out your datasheets, pick up your soldering iron, and remember why you're here. Life brings you into this world, entropy takes you out. In between you get a handful of seconds to be passionate. And at that second daily visit of yourself, you look at her across the sink and, together, you smile. Most people dream when they're asleep, but not you. You dream when you're awake. And you thrive on it.
Neat. When you encounter nonsense, you struggle to make sense of it. Pedantic? Sometimes. The last time you looked at the clock it was four in the morning and you were just getting started. Flow. You know you're winning when your total being is a stream of consciousness that can only be expressed through your fingers. Your thinking is esoteric and your biggest fear is stagnation. What happens when your heart is still beating but your brain has stopped? You're determined never to find out. When you visit your family you see a directed acyclic graph, and someone asks you why you're smiling. "I'm just happy." Understading is expensive, but you think it's worth it every single time. Your future isn't programmable but you're damn well going to try. The worst case scenario is catastrophic failure. Is that worse than not trying at all? Right now you don't think so, and dear God you hope you're right.
Most people see function, you see art. You like spaces. You like enclosing them and moving them around. Elegance and cleverness make you thrill in a way that you can't fully understand. You don't solve problems, you wait for the solutions to appear in your head. You trust your brain to take care of it, but fundamentally it's a black box. Experience, intuition, magic. Will it run out? As long as a force through a distance can actually get some work done, it won't. Not for you. Your mind depends on it, and you're built too well to break. An error of 2mm over 20 feet makes your parasympathetic nervous system go into overdrive and that's why you're here. Thinking isn't enough.
